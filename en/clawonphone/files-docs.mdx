## Using Files and Documents via Voice

By talking to Veta, you can search for files in Google Drive, create documents with Google Docs, and check or modify data in Google Sheets.

<Note>
To use this feature, you must have **Google Account Connection**. If you haven't connected it yet, please press the G button to connect your Google account first.
</Note>

## Drive - Searching for Files

You can search for files saved in your Google Drive by name or keywords.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Search by File Name" icon="magnifying-glass">
**"Find the quote file."**

**"Search for the February report file."**
</Card>

<Card title="Search by Type/Keyword" icon="file">
**"Find a recently created spreadsheet."**

**"Are there any documents related to Taejun Pharmaceuticals?"**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

Veta will share the search results along with the file names and modification dates. Once you find the desired file, you can immediately open it by saying, "Open that file."

## Docs - Reading and Drafting Documents

You can check the content of Google Docs or create a new document entirely.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Reading Documents" icon="file-lines">
**"Read the content of the project proposal."**

**"Show me the meeting minutes we created yesterday."**
</Card>

<Card title="Drafting Documents" icon="file-pen">
**"Create a meeting minutes document."**

**"Draft a document for the February sales report."**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

## Conversational Document Drafting

You can draft document content step-by-step through a conversation with Veta.

<Steps>

<Step title="Request to draft a document">
**Me**: "Create a meeting minutes document."

**Veta**: "What content should I include? Please let me know the meeting date and the attendees."
</Step>

<Step title="Provide the details">
**Me**: "It's this morning's team meeting, and the attendees are me, Manager Kim, and Director Lee. The main agenda is the February sales review."

**Veta**: "I have created the meeting minutes document. The title is 'February 23rd Team Meeting Minutes'."
</Step>

</Steps>

## Sheets - Reading and Writing Spreadsheets

You can read data from Google Sheets or insert new data.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Reading Data" icon="table">
**"Read the sales sheet."**

**"Show me the inventory status spreadsheet."**
</Card>

<Card title="Writing Data" icon="pen-to-square">
**"Add this month's performance to the sales sheet."**

**"Update the inventory sheet."**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

Veta will read the data from the sheet and summarize the contents for you. When adding data, Veta will verify exactly what values to insert through the conversation.

## More Speaking Examples

<CardGroup cols={1}>

<Card title="File and Document Commands" icon="folder-open">
**"Find the Excel file in Google Drive."**

**"Open the document for last month's sales report."**
</Card>

<Card title="Reading Specific Data" icon="folder-open">
**"Read the data in column A from the order form sheet."**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

<Tip>
It's okay if you don't remember the exact file name. Just say keywords like "quote" or "report," and Veta will find all the relevant files.
</Tip>

<Tip>
When reading data from Sheets, you can specify a range like "from A1 to D10" to view only the precise section you want. If you don't specify a range, Veta will summarize the entire content for you.
</Tip>
