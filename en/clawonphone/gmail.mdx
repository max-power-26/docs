## Using Email with Your Voice

By talking to Veta, you can search for emails in Gmail or draft and send new emails. You can handle this with just a few words, without needing to open the app or type.

<Note>
To use this feature, you must have **Google Account Connection**. If you haven't connected it yet, please press the G button to connect your Google account first.
</Note>

## Searching Emails

You can find the email you want in your inbox. You can freely search by sender, subject, or keywords.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Search by Sender" icon="user">
**"Find the email sent by Manager Lee."**

**"Show me the email from Taejun Pharmaceuticals."**
</Card>

<Card title="Search by Keyword" icon="magnifying-glass">
**"Find the quote email."**

**"Search for the order-related email I received last week."**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

When Veta reads out the search results, you can see the subject, sender, and date. If you're curious about the contents of a specific email, you can follow up by saying, "Show me the content of that email."

## Sending Emails

You can draft and send new emails. Just say the recipient, subject, and body, and Veta will compose the email.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Send a Simple Email" icon="paper-plane">
**"Send the meeting minutes email to Director Kim."**

**"Send a 'Confirmed' email to Manager Lee."**
</Card>

<Card title="Send a Detailed Email" icon="envelope-open-text">
**"Send an email to Manager Kim at Taejun Pharmaceuticals, attaching the February quote."**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

## Conversational Email Drafting

You don't have to state the entire email content at once. You can draft it step-by-step while conversing with Veta.

<Steps>

<Step title="Request to send an email">
**Me**: "Send an email to Director Kim."

**Veta**: "What content should I include?"
</Step>

<Step title="Provide the content">
**Me**: "Tell him I am sending the sales performance report for this month."

**Veta**: "Shall I send an email to Director Kim with the subject 'February Sales Performance Report Delivery'?"
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm and send">
**Me**: "Yes, send it."

**Veta**: "The email has been sent to Director Kim."
</Step>

</Steps>

<Tip>
You only need to say the recipient's name; Veta will find their email address from your contacts and send the email. Just saying "To Director Kim" is enough.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Veta will always verify the content before sending it. When it asks, "May I send it?", review the details and simply say, "Send it" to finalize the sending process.
</Tip>
