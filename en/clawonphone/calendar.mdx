## Managing Your Schedule with Voice

By talking to Veta, you can check your schedule on Google Calendar or create new events. You can manage your calendar with just a few words without opening the calendar app.

<Note>
To use this feature, you must have **Google Account Connection**. If you haven't connected it yet, please press the G button to connect your Google account first.
</Note>

## Checking Your Schedule

You can check your schedule for any desired time frame, such as today, tomorrow, or this week.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Today's Schedule" icon="calendar-day">
**"What's on my schedule today?"**

**"Do I have any appointments this afternoon?"**
</Card>

<Card title="Specific Date Schedule" icon="calendar">
**"Tell me my schedule for tomorrow."**

**"What's going on this Friday?"**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

Veta will organize and read out the events for that date in chronological order. If there are no events, it will inform you, "You have no events scheduled."

## Creating an Event

You can immediately add a new event using your voice. Just state the date, time, and the name of the event.

**Speaking Examples**

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Adding a Simple Event" icon="calendar-plus">
**"Schedule a team meeting at 2 PM tomorrow."**

**"Add a client visit at 10 AM on Friday."**
</Card>

<Card title="Adding a Specific Event" icon="calendar-check">
**"Schedule a performance report meeting from 3 PM to 4 PM on March 5th."**

**"Schedule a one-on-one with the team leader at 9 AM next Monday."**
</Card>

</CardGroup>

## Conversational Event Creation

You don't need to provide all the information at once. Veta will ask for any missing details.

<Steps>

<Step title="Request to add an event">
**Me**: "Schedule an event for me."

**Veta**: "What kind of event is it? Please also tell me the date and time."
</Step>

<Step title="Provide the details">
**Me**: "Taejun Pharmaceuticals meeting at 2 PM tomorrow."

**Veta**: "Shall I add a 'Taejun Pharmaceuticals meeting' event for 2 PM tomorrow, February 24th?"
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm to complete">
**Me**: "Yes, add it."

**Veta**: "I have added the 'Taejun Pharmaceuticals meeting' event for 2 PM tomorrow."
</Step>

</Steps>

<Tip>
When you state a date using natural language like "tomorrow," "next Monday," or "March 5th," Veta understands it perfectly. You don't need to speak in a strict date format.
</Tip>

<Tip>
If you try to add an event without mentioning a time, Veta will ask, "What time should I schedule it?" You can finalize the details one by one through the conversation.
</Tip>
