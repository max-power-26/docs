## What is Google Account Connection?

To use Google services such as Gmail, Calendar, and Drive within ClawOnPhone, you must **connect your Google account**. Once connected, Veta can access your account to handle tasks like checking emails, managing schedules, and searching for files on your behalf.

<Warning>
If you do not connect your Google account, you cannot use features related to Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Sheets, Docs, Contacts, Tasks, Forms, and Slides. You only need to connect your account once.
</Warning>

## How to Connect

<Steps>

<Step title="Tap the G Button">
Press the **G button** at the top of the ClawOnPhone screen. The Google login pop-up will appear.
</Step>

<Step title="Select Your Google Account">
Select the Google account you wish to use. You can connect either a company Google Workspace account or a personal Gmail account.
</Step>

<Step title="Grant Permissions">
A list of services that ClawOnPhone wants to access will be displayed. Please tap the **"Allow"** button. You must grant permission for Veta to use these services.
</Step>

<Step title="Connection Complete">
When the G button changes to an active state, the connection is complete. You can now use voice commands like "Check my email" or "Tell me today's schedule."
</Step>

</Steps>

## Is My Data Secure?

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Access to Your Account Only" icon="lock">
Veta will only access the **Google account that is connected**. It will never access the data of other users.
</Card>

<Card title="Task-specific Authentication" icon="shield-check">
We use standard Google OAuth authentication. Every time an email is sent or an event is scheduled, it is executed strictly with the permissions of your account.
</Card>

</CardGroup>

<Tip>
If your company uses Google Workspace, we highly recommend connecting your company account. You'll gain instant access to your work email, calendar, and drive.
</Tip>

<Note>
If you wish to disconnect, you can select 'Disconnect Google' from the Settings menu. After disconnecting, you will need to reconnect to use Google-related features again.
</Note>
