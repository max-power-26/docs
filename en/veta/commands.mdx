## Try Speaking Like This

Veta understands natural language. You don't need to memorize stiff commands; just speak naturally as you would when asking a colleague for a favor.

Below are examples by category. Even if you don't type them exactly as written, Veta will understand you as long as the meaning is similar.

## Converse Naturally

<Tip>
  You don't need to memorize the sentences written above. You can say things comfortably in your own expressions like "Check my email," "Calendar check," or "What do I have tomorrow," and Veta will handle it.
</Tip>

## Conversing Step-by-Step

Veta remembers the previous conversation for up to 30 turns. You don't have to say everything at once; you can refine your request as the conversation progresses.

For instance, you could converse like this:

**Me:** "Tell me my schedule for this week."

**Veta:** (Shows the schedule list for the week)

**Me:** "Wednesday afternoon looks free. Schedule a team meeting for 3 PM."

**Veta:** "I have scheduled the team meeting for 3 PM on Wednesday."

**Me:** "Add Director Kim and Manager Lee as meeting attendees."

**Veta:** "The attendees have been added."

Through this, Veta understands the context of the previous conversation and processes follow-up requests.

<Note>
  To use Google-related commands (Email, Calendar, Files, Contacts, etc.), you must first connect your Google account in the ClawOnPhone app.
</Note>
